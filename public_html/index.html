Simplified Hanzi · Component-First

Learn to Read Chinese Characters

Most characters aren't arbitrary shapes to memorize — they're 2–4 known pieces stacked together, often with a meaning clue built in. This course teaches those pieces first, so each new character you meet is mostly already familiar.

Lessons

  • Lesson 1 人 木 水 口 日 Building Blocks: Your First Five Components Pictographs that appear inside hundreds of other characters — and two that change shape when they move inside a compound.
  • Lesson 2 女 子 Woman and Child: 女 / 子 Two high-frequency components, plus why 子 often means nothing at all as a suffix.
  • Lesson 3 大 小 中 Big, Small, Middle: 大 / 小 / 中 Exactly what's printed on every drink, snack, and clothing size — you'll be able to test these immediately.
  • Lesson 4 上 下 出 入 Up, Down, Out, In: 上 / 下 / 出 / 入 Indicative characters — diagrams of a relationship rather than pictures of a thing — and the exit/entrance signs you'll use everywhere.
  • Lesson 5 一 二 三 十 Counting to Ten (Almost): 一 / 二 / 三 / 十 Three are tally marks. The fourth, 十, lets you read every number from 10 to 99 without learning anything new.
  • Lesson 6 四 五 六 七 八 九 Counting to Ten, Completed: 四 / 五 / 六 / 七 / 八 / 九 The remaining six digits — none follow the tally pattern, and one has a documented reason for breaking it.
  • Lesson 7 山 火 土 Mountain, Fire, Earth: 山 / 火 / 土 Four of the five classical elements are now complete. 山 closes two loops from earlier lessons; 火 doubles into a blaze.
  • Lesson 8 月 天 年 Moon, Sky, Year: 月 / 天 / 年 With all digits in hand, three more characters let you read and write complete dates in Chinese.
  • Lesson 9 百 千 Hundred, Thousand: 百 / 千 Two characters that take you into the numbers you actually need for prices, hotel rates, and train tickets.
  • Lesson 10 金 贝 Metal, Shell: Money Vocabulary — 金 / 贝 The fifth classical element closes out, and the "shell/cowrie" component explains a cluster of modern money vocabulary.
  • Lesson 11 手 心 Hand, Heart: 手 / 心 Two of the script's most productive semantic components, covering physical action and emotion — plus their contracted left-side forms.
  • Lesson 12 言 讠 Speech: 言 / 讠 One of the script's most productive semantic components, built on 口 — sits inside almost every character about talking, asking, and naming.
